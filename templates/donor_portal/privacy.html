1. Information We Collect

At BloodLink, we collect and process certain personal information to provide our blood donation services. This policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and your rights regarding your data.

  • Name and Contact Details: We collect your full name, email address, phone number, physical address, and blood type when you register as a donor.
  • Medical Information: We collect your date of birth and gender to verify eligibility for donation.
  • Donation History: We maintain records of your donations, including dates, locations, and blood types donated.
  • Communication Records: We track SMS notifications sent to you and your responses to emergency blood requests.

2. How We Use Your Information

Your information is used to:

  • Provide Donation Services: Match donors with patients who need blood transfusions.
  • Send Emergency Notifications: Alert registered donors when their blood type is urgently needed.
  • Maintain Donation Records: Track your donation history and eligibility for future donations.
  • Communicate with Donors: Send SMS notifications and updates about blood requests and donation opportunities.
  • Improve Our Services: Analyze usage patterns to enhance our matching algorithms and user experience.
  • Ensure Compliance: Verify donor eligibility and maintain accurate records for regulatory compliance.

3. Information Sharing

We may share your information in the following circumstances:

  • With Healthcare Providers: When you respond to an emergency blood request, we share your contact information and blood type with the requesting hospital or healthcare facility.
  • With Blood Banks: We may collaborate with other blood banks and donation centers to optimize blood availability and distribution.
  • For Research and Analytics: We use anonymized data for research purposes to improve our services and understand donation trends.
  • As Required by Law: We may disclose your information when required by law, court order, or government regulation.

4. Data Security

We implement industry-standard security measures to protect your personal information:

  • Encryption: All data transmission is encrypted using secure protocols.
  • Access Controls: Only authorized personnel have access to your personal information.
  • Secure Storage: Your data is stored on secure servers with regular security updates.
  • Regular Security Audits: We conduct periodic security assessments to identify and address potential vulnerabilities.

5. Your Rights

You have the following rights regarding your personal information:

  • Access and Correction: You can access, review, and update your personal information at any time. You can request deletion of your account and personal data.
  • Consent Management: You can control what types of communications you receive and can withdraw consent for certain data processing activities.
  • Data Portability: You can request a copy of your personal data in a machine-readable format.
  • Complaint Resolution: You have the right to file a complaint with data protection authorities if you believe your privacy rights have been violated.

6. Contact Information

If you have questions about this Privacy Policy or how we handle your personal information, please contact us:

  • Email: privacy@bloodlink.org

7. Policy Updates

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, technology, or legal requirements. The most current version will always be available on our website.

Significant changes will be communicated through our website or email notifications.
